Foundation — the recognition gap
Because of gaps in testing and diagnostics for Long COVID, most people would reasonably attribute a new chronic condition to something else — and never connect it to COVID.
Official surveillance counts ~15M. This model implies 70M ever developed one — a recognition gap of ~55M never connected to COVID.
How to read this. The headline rate (top) is what Tian measured — no extrapolation. Everything below depends on assumptions the data can't settle, so you set them. The study itself says resolution "cannot be reliably determined,"1 so its count is people ever identified, not currently active — which is exactly why persistence is a slider, anchored to how chronic conditions behave generally. Self-report is shown as a floor, never a cap, because recognizing Long COVID requires knowing you have it.
Figures are modeled estimates with wide uncertainty, not point facts. No never-infected comparison group, so these are not "excess over background." For exploration and discussion.
